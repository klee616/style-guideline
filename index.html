Logo and Variants

Pocket Pals feature two marmots together as its logo. The reason we chose marmots was because it's a friendly and cute animal making it approuchable to a younger audience. Furthermore, it also represents BC (where the Vancouver Island Marmots are found) and creates a local sense of pride. In addition, the VI marmots are an endangered species which relates to the purpose of the app.

We chose Heavitas for its primative simplistic shape as users would look at it and associate the shape language with learning blocks used in elementry school. Its boldness also makes it easy for users to read and recognise what the title is. Although blocky, it does have a certain roundness to the corners which makes it less intimidating and harsh.

Color

Fringy Flower

#B9E4C9

Fringy Flower is being used to create harmony and peace.
Example: Its used as our primary theme to help users feel relaxed while navigating, quiz's, and reading articles.
Green in design can create balance, harmony, and stability. It fosters a calm and peaceful atmosphere, aiding users in feeling centered and focused, thus enhancing their ability to absorb and retain information in an educational setting

Green Pea

#13644D

Green Pea helps with gaining a connection to nature.
Example: its being used as our top bar, which shows users how our dark and light greens are connect with each other, to create a theme of nature and calmness.
Dark green reminds us of trees and forests, making us think of nature and feeling calm. It connects us to the outdoors, even when we're inside, creating a peaceful atmosphere. Using dark green in designs can help people feel relaxed and closer to nature.

Sunglow

#FFC53A

Sunglow creates hopefulness that our animals will be saved.
Example: It’s being used as our bottom bar, where our graphics of animals are highlighted with this color, for creativity, and hopefulness.
The bright and cheerful color of sunglow can represent looking forward to good things. It can make people feel hopeful and positive about what's to come, which is perfect for designs that inspire and motivate.

Mango Tango

Mango Tango creates confidence and boldness that these animals will be saved.
Example: It’s being used as our bottom bar, where our sunglow and mango tango color themes connect to create optimism.
The bold color of Mango Tango shows confidence and makes a strong statement in design. It grabs attention and leaves a memorable impression.

Color Contrast Ratio

Green Pea with a white font matches with accessibility, because there is a hierarchy with dark colors not overshadowing the white text, it allows the white text to stand out more, and increases readability.

Fringy Flower background with a black font helps with contrast and, people with disabilities can sense easily tell see which direction the words are telling them to do.

With all the colors being consistence depending on which font they use, it helps users build trust in our app that we are reliable and safe.

Using colors, like orange helps identity which cue is the action taking place. Orange meaning confidence and boldness that its important action in our app.

Icon Graphic

We have 5 icons that help users navigate through our website. Our icons are a mix of vector and svg format to help users feel comfortable. Some are found https://pictogrammers.com/library/mdi/

We decided to use our Logo has our Home button for consistency with our top bar and login page showing what the logo means. Clarity also helps users comfortability.

We decided, on vector based icon that’s playful for our target audience, and is easily remember able for cognitive disabilities. Its style is based on old fashion cartoons.

Our play Button is, an interesting icon, we made it using vector, and were trying to be empathic with our users being playful, and funny.




Setting shouldn't reinvent the wheel, its a type of icon that we want to use clarity for our users to be comfortable. Our icon is SVG because there unerivale icon out there.

Our Hamburger Menu, is another SVG that has hierocracy and clarity values. The nature of the menu is always on top right side because usually there a navigation systems on the left, we have our home button the left, and we decide on this icon because its universal, and gives users trust in our app.

We specifically chosen this icon for our text-to-speech option because we use our ears to hear. So when users see this icon they'll associate it with listening therefore make the connection that it is related to audio. Since this will be use in the article sections users will know that it is for reading out the text.
